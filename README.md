🏭 DEPZ Air Quality Analysis — Bangladesh (2019–2023)
Ground-level air quality time series analysis for the Dhaka Export Processing Zone (DEPZ), Bangladesh — combining in-situ pollutant measurements with NASA meteorological reanalysis data to quantify seasonal patterns, COVID-19 lockdown effects, and 2024 forecasts.

📍 Study Area
Location: Dhaka Export Processing Zone (DEPZ), Ashulia, Bangladesh
Coordinates: 23.94821°N, 90.27727°E
Period: January 2019 – December 2023 (60 monthly observations)
Pollutants: PM2.5 (µg/m³), PM10 (µg/m³), CO (ppm)
DEPZ is one of Bangladesh's largest export processing zones, housing ~500 garment and manufacturing factories employing approximately 80,000 workers. It sits ~25 km northwest of Dhaka city centre in the Turag River floodplain.

🔬 Key Findings
1. COVID-19 lockdown caused a 56% reduction in PM2.5
During the Wave 1 lockdown (March–August 2020), DEPZ factories were completely shut. PM2.5 annual mean dropped from 52.4 µg/m³ (2019) to 23.2 µg/m³ (2020) — a 56% reduction. CO showed no weather correlation (r ≈ 0, p > 0.25), confirming the drop was industrial in origin, not weather-driven.

3. Monsoon drives a 4× seasonal suppression of PM2.5
July PM2.5 averages ~12 µg/m³ vs January ~75 µg/m³. Humidity (r = −0.50) and rainfall (r = −0.43) are the dominant suppressants. PM10 shows even stronger humidity coupling (r = −0.61).
4. CO is a weather-independent industrial activity proxy
CO shows near-zero correlation with all weather variables (temperature r = −0.15, p = 0.25; wind r = −0.01, p = 0.93; rainfall r = +0.009, p = 0.94). This makes CO a reliable signal for separating industrial vs meteorological contributions to PM variation.
